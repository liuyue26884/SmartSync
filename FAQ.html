FAQ

How often should I get my eyes checked for a new prescription?

It is recommended to have an eye exam at least every two years for adults and annually for children or those with existing eye conditions. However, if you notice a change in your vision, schedule an appointment with an optometrist or ophthalmologist sooner.

What are reading glasses?

Reading glasses are non-prescription eyewear designed to assist people with presbyopia, a common age-related condition where the eyes lose their ability to focus on close-up objects. They are available in various strengths and can be purchased over-the-counter.

How do I clean and care for my eyeglasses?

To clean your eyeglasses, use a microfiber cleaning cloth to remove smudges and dirt. Avoid using tissues or paper towels, as they can scratch the lenses. You can also use a gentle eyeglass cleaner. Store your glasses in a protective case when not in use to prevent damage.

What are the benefits of wearing sunglasses?

Sunglasses offer protection from harmful UV rays, reduce glare, and shield your eyes from dust, wind, and debris. They can also help prevent conditions like cataracts and macular degeneration.

Can I wear contact lenses instead of glasses?

Contact lenses are an alternative to glasses for vision correction. However, whether you can wear them depends on your eye health, prescription, and personal preferences. Consult with an eye care professional to determine if contacts are suitable for you.

How do I choose the right frame for my face shape?

The best frame shape for your face depends on your face shape. Generally, rectangular frames complement round faces, while round or oval frames work well for square faces. Oval faces can often wear various frame shapes. It's also essential to consider your personal style and comfort.

Do I need to wear protective eyewear for sports or specific activities?

Yes, protective eyewear is crucial for sports and activities that pose a risk to your eyes. Sports goggles or safety glasses are designed to withstand impact and protect your eyes from injury. Always use appropriate eyewear for your chosen activity.

Can I get designer eyeglasses with prescription lenses?

Yes, many designer brands offer eyeglasses with prescription lenses. You can have your prescription added to a wide range of designer frames to combine style and functionality.

Can I use my old eyeglass frames with a new prescription?

In some cases, you can replace the lenses in your existing frames with new prescription lenses if they are in good condition and suitable for your new prescription. However, it's essential to consult your optometrist or optician to determine if this is possible.

Are anti-reflective coatings worth it for my eyeglasses?

Anti-reflective coatings can reduce glare, improve night vision, and make your lenses more cosmetically appealing by reducing reflections on the surface. They are often recommended, especially if you work on a computer or drive at night.

What are the differences between single-vision, bifocal, and progressive lenses?

Single-vision lenses have a single prescription throughout the entire lens. Bifocal lenses have two distinct prescriptions for distance and near vision. Progressive lenses offer a smooth transition between distance and near vision without visible lines.

Can I wear contact lenses and eyeglasses interchangeably?

Yes, many people choose to wear both contact lenses and eyeglasses depending on their activities and preferences. However, it's crucial to follow your eye care professional's recommendations for proper usage.

What should I do if my eyeglasses break or become damaged?

If your eyeglasses break or get damaged, avoid trying to fix them yourself. Take them to an eyewear professional like us for proper repairs.

Can children wear contact lenses, or should they stick to eyeglasses?

Children can wear contact lenses, but it depends on their age, maturity level, and individual eye health. Consult with a pediatric optometrist to determine if contact lenses are suitable for your child.

How can I prevent my eyeglasses from constantly slipping down my nose?

To prevent slipping, consider getting glasses with adjustable nose pads or grippy temple tips. An optician can also make adjustments to the frame for a more secure fit.

Can I wear blue light-blocking lenses to reduce digital eye strain from screens?

Yes, blue light-blocking lenses can help reduce eye strain from prolonged screen use. They are available with or without a prescription and can be added to your eyeglasses.

What is the difference between reading glasses and prescription eyeglasses?

Reading glasses are typically used for close-up tasks and have the same prescription in both lenses, whereas prescription eyeglasses can correct various vision problems and have customized prescriptions for each eye.

Do I need to wear eyeglasses all the time if my prescription is for distance vision only?

If your prescription is for distance vision only, you may not need to wear your glasses all the time. However, follow your eye care professional's advice regarding when and how often to wear them.

Are there any specific eyewear considerations for individuals with allergies or dry eyes?

People with allergies or dry eyes should look for hypoallergenic frame materials and consider lenses with coatings that reduce glare and protect against dryness and irritants.

How long does it take to adjust to progressive lenses if I've never worn them before?

The adjustment period for progressive lenses varies from person to person. It can take a few days to a couple of weeks to adapt fully to the seamless transition between different prescription strengths.
